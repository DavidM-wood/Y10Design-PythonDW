The Evolution of the Hockey Stick

The evolution of the hockey stick will start in the 1800s and go to 2020

  • 1800s-1920s:

    - Hockey sticks were hand-carved from birch.

    - In 1920, sticks were now two pieces. The blade was seperate from the shaft.
  • 1926:

    - Cy Denneny was the first profesional hockey player to use a curved stick in a game.
  • 1950s:

    - Manufactures started to wrap the blade in fiberglass to add strength and make them lighter.
  • 1960s:

    - Manufactures started to wrap the shaft in fiberglass. This made them lighter, stronger, and cheaper to produce.

    - Bobby Hull and Stan Mikita were the first to popularize curved sticks.
  • 1970s:

    - Sher-Wood started to dominate the manufacturing business by making sticks made of aspen. These sticks were more durable.
  • 1980s:

    - First stick made of aluminum was made. They were more durable and lightweight.
  • 1990s:

    - Sticks made of aluminum were made popular by Wayne Gretzky.
  • 1995:

    - The first composite blade was made. They were lighter, flexible , and players were able to score more goals with them. Composite sticks were known to break easily because of the flexible properties.
  • 2000s:

    - Easton developed the first one piece composite stick. Shots became harder, more accurate, and more deceptive.
  • 2010s:

    - By 2010, every player in the NHL had switched to composite sticks.
  • Today:

    - Sticks today are very light with the lightest stick being as light as 350g.

    - Blades flex to give extra power.

    - The shape of the shafts have changed to different shapes.

    - Sticks are made out of carbon-fibres and carbon-weaves.
